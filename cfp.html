Call for Presentations

Share your research on computational and statistical approaches to text at Text as Data, October 5, 2026.

Overview

We invite submissions for oral presentations and posters on topics related to text-as-data research. We welcome work from political science, sociology, economics, linguistics, computer science, communication, and related fields.

Topics of Interest

Submissions may address, but are not limited to:

  • Natural language processing and computational linguistics
  • Topic modeling, classification, and information extraction
  • Large language models and their applications in social science
  • Measurement and validation with textual data
  • Corpus construction, digitization, and data collection
  • Applications to news, social media, legislation, archives, and other text sources
  • Ethics, bias, and reproducibility in text-as-data research

Submission Format

Please prepare an abstract of up to 500 words describing your contribution. Include the title, author names and affiliations, and whether you are submitting for a talk or poster. Submission instructions and the submission portal will be posted here soon.
